= Carbonara: A Brief History
:toc:
:toc-title: Contents

== Origins

Carbonara is a pasta dish from *Rome*, first documented in the early 1950s.
Its exact origin is debated, but the most widely accepted theory links it to
the postwar period when American soldiers brought bacon and powdered eggs to
central Italy.

== Name

The name _carbonara_ derives from _carbone_ (charcoal).
One popular theory suggests that charcoal workers (_carbonari_) in the
Apennine Mountains invented it as a hearty on-the-road meal.

== Key Ingredients

[cols="1,2"]
|===
| Ingredient | Role

| Guanciale
| Cured pork cheek; provides fat and a rich, slightly sweet flavour.

| Egg yolks
| Form the creamy sauce when emulsified with pasta water.

| Pecorino Romano
| Hard sheep's milk cheese; adds saltiness and depth.

| Black pepper
| Gives the dish its characteristic bite.
|===

== Common Mistakes

* Using cream — the authentic recipe contains none.
* Scrambling the eggs by adding sauce over direct heat.
* Substituting regular bacon, which lacks the flavour of guanciale.

== Modern Variations

While traditionalists insist on strict adherence to the original four
ingredients, variations exist worldwide — from adding garlic and onion to
using heavy cream.
These are often referred to as _carbonara-style_ dishes rather than
true carbonara.
