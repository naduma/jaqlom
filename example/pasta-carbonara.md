# Pasta Carbonara

A classic Roman pasta dish. The creamy sauce comes entirely from eggs and cheese — no cream needed.

## Ingredients (serves 2)

| Ingredient | Amount |
|------------|--------|
| Spaghetti | 200 g |
| Guanciale (or pancetta) | 80 g |
| Egg yolks | 3 |
| Pecorino Romano, grated | 40 g |
| Black pepper | to taste |
| Salt | to taste |

## Steps

1. **Boil the pasta** — Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente. Reserve a ladle of pasta water before draining.
2. **Cook the guanciale** — Fry diced guanciale in a pan over medium heat until the fat renders and the edges are crisp. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
3. **Make the sauce** — In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks, Pecorino, and a generous amount of black pepper. Slowly add the reserved pasta water while stirring to temper the eggs.
4. **Combine** — Add the drained pasta to the pan with guanciale. Pour the sauce over the pasta off the heat and toss quickly. Add pasta water as needed to loosen.
5. **Serve** — Plate immediately and finish with extra Pecorino and black pepper.

> **Key tip** — Never apply heat when adding the egg sauce. Residual warmth from the pasta is enough.
